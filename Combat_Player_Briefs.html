How every roll works the whole dice system on one screen

1 · Rolling

Roll 3 dice. Total equal to or under your number = success. How far under is your margin (it decides extra hits, who wins a contest, and how hard a power lands).

  • 3–4 = automatic critical success (the best result).
  • 5 crits if your number is 15+; 6 crits if 16+.
  • 17 fails (a critical fail if your number is under 16); 18 always critical-fails.
  • Miss by 10+ = critical fail.

2 · Defending

Attacked? Pick one defence and roll 3d6 against it. Make it = the hit is avoided.

  • Dodge — always available vs anything you can see.
  • Parry — melee weapon in hand (a lit sabre Parries blaster bolts).
  • Block — shield or gauntlet ready.
  • Retreat a step while defending: +3 Dodge, +1 Parry/Block (sabre +3).

3 · Damage & armour the part new players miss

On a hit, roll the weapon's dice. Then the target's DR (armour) is subtracted — but first the weapon's Armour Divisor (AD) divides that DR.

Worked: 6d bolt rolls 21, weapon is AD 2, target armour DR 18. AD 2 → armour counts as 9. Damage through = 21 − 9 = 12.

A lightsabre is AD 5 — DR 20 becomes 4. That's why blades cut through nearly anything.

4 · Common modifiers applied to your number before you roll

  • Target behind cover: −2 to −5.
  • You moved & attacked: −2 (and the shot/swing caps at skill 9).
  • Aiming at a body part: skull −7, vitals −3, arm/leg −2, hand/foot −4, face −5.
  • Deceptive Attack: every −2 you take = −1 to the foe's defence.

Casting the Force your engine — read this first

Every power is “Roll Force”: roll 3d6 against the attribute the power uses, then pay its FP.

  • Will 35 (Nightsister & Will powers) — you only fail on a 17 or 18. Treat these as automatic.
  • IQ 18 (Concordance & Living Path) — fails only on ~16+.
  • ST 13 / DX 10 — the push/pull/move powers.

Your real limit is 69 FP, not the dice. Spend it; it comes back with rest.

Resisted powers = a Quick Contest: your attribute vs the target's (Will/HT/ST as the row says); you win ties on most.

Worked — Hex of Agony: spend 4 FP for −4. Contest Will 35 vs their Will (say 12): you roll vs 35, they roll vs 12 — you win almost always → −4 to all their DX & IQ rolls.

Compartmentalized Mind lets you hold a mental action and act in the same turn.

Gunslinger — read before you shoot it changes your number on every shot

You add your weapon's full Accuracy to every shot for free — no Aim manoeuvre, and no penalty for snap or unready shots. A normal shooter must spend a whole turn aiming to add Acc; you just add it. So your number is always skill + Accuracy, then you adjust for range and the situation.

Anatomy of a shot follow these 4 steps every time

1Build your number Start with your skill, add Accuracy (free, Gunslinger), add the rapid-fire bonus, then subtract range and any aim-at-a-part penalty.
2Roll 3d6 Equal to or under the number = you're on target. How far under is your margin.
3Count the hits Your guns fire bursts. Extra bolts hit = margin ÷ Recoil, rounded down. So 1 hit plus one more for each full Recoil you beat the number by.
4Roll damage, take off armour Roll the dice per bolt. The Armour Divisor shrinks the target's DR first, then subtract DR. What's left is the wound.

Full worked example — rifle, trooper 100 m away, you spray full-auto:
Step 1: 31 skill + 6 Acc + 2 (RoF 10) − 10 (range 100 m) = 27.  Step 2: you roll 11 → margin = 27 − 11 = 16.  Step 3: 16 ÷ Recoil 4 = 4, so 1 + 4 = 5 bolts hit.  Step 4: each bolt 6d (avg 21), AD 2 halves the trooper's DR 18 to 9, so 21 − 9 = 12 per bolt — about 60 damage lands.
