How every roll works the only dice rule you need

Roll 3d6. Equal to or under your number = success. Lower roll = bigger margin (margin matters for damage spill, contests, and how well a Force power lands).

  • 3–4 always succeeds — and is a critical (best possible result).
  • 5 crits if your number is 15+, 6 crits if 16+.
  • 17 fails (critical fail if your number is under 16); 18 is always a critical fail.
  • Any roll that misses by 10+ is also a critical fail.

Getting attacked? Pick one active defence and roll 3d6 against it:

  • Dodge — always available, works against anything you can see.
  • Parry — with a melee weapon in hand (a lit sabre can Parry blaster bolts).
  • Block — with a shield/gauntlet ready.
  • Retreat 1 step as you defend: +3 Dodge, +1 Parry/Block (sabre = +3).

Common to-hit mods: target behind cover −2 to −5 · you moved & attacked −2 (skill capped 9) · aim at a body part: torso 0, arm/leg −2, hand/foot −4, head −5, eye/vitals −3 to −7.

Casting the Force your engine — read this first

Every power is “Roll Force”: roll 3d6 against the governing attribute listed on the power, then pay its FP. Your numbers:

  • Will 35 — Nightsister & Will powers. Essentially automatic; you fail only on a natural 17–18.
  • IQ 18 — Concordance & Living-Path powers. Still very reliable.
  • ST 13 / DX 10 — the few push/pull/movement powers.

You have 69 FP — a huge tank. That's the real limit on how much you can do per fight, not the dice.

Resisted powers are a Quick Contest: your attribute vs the target's (Will, HT, ST, etc. as the row says). You win ties on most; the table notes who resists with what.

Concentrate powers take your turn (1 second) and can be spoiled if you're hit — your Compartmentalized Mind lets you hold a second mental action in parallel.

Bolster: many Inscribed powers can be over-charged for extra FP (the bigger number on your full sheet) for stronger effect.

Gunslinger — read this before you shoot it changes every shot

You get your weapon's full Accuracy on every shot without taking an Aim manoeuvre, and you ignore the penalties for snap/unready shots. In plain terms: a normal gunner has to spend a turn aiming to add Acc — you just add it, always. So your effective number is skill + Acc, then apply range and situation.

How to shoot — the maths, with numbers your core loop

SituationWhat you roll
Range penalty
from the Size/Speed-Range table		 Subtract for distance: 10 m −4 · 20 m −6 · 50 m −8 · 100 m −10 · 200 m −12 · 500 m −15. Inside the rifle's 600 m half-range, damage is full.
Rapid fire
your guns spit a lot of bolts		 High RoF gives a flat to-hit bonus: RoF 10 → +2, RoF 15–16 → +3 (already in the rolls above). Then recoil turns overkill into extra hits: for every full Rcl you beat the number by, +1 bolt hits. Rifle Rcl 4: beat by 4 = 2 hits, by 8 = 3 hits…
Worked example Rifle at a trooper 100 m off: 37 −10 range = 27. You roll a 19 — you beat 27 by 8, so with Rcl 4 that's three 6d(2) hits landing. Each averages 21, divisor 2 vs his DR.
Suppression
repeating blaster		 Spray a zone (RoF 15+): everyone crossing it must Dodge or risk a bolt; great for pinning a doorway or covering the others' move.
Aim at a part Targeted: head −5, vitals/eye −3 to −7, limb −2, weapon-hand to disarm −4. You have skill to spare — called shots punch through weak points.
Move & shoot Move-and-Attack caps the shot at skill 9 and −2 (Gunslinger keeps your Acc but not the cap). Prefer to plant, fire, then reposition.
