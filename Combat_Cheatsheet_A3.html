ATK Making an Attack — the Universal Roll

Every attack is 3d6, roll-under. Work out your effective skill, roll 3d6, and you hit if the roll is ≤ that number. Low is good — a 3 or 4 is always spectacular.

Effective skillBase weapon / unarmed skill + bonuses − penalties (posture, Deceptive, range, cover, light…). Melee mods here; ranged → Page 2.
Hit / missRoll ≤ skill = hit. Roll > skill = miss.
MarginEffective skill − your roll. Drives burst hits, Feints & locks.
Who defendsTarget gets one free active defence per attack (a Reaction). Extra defences that turn: −4 each (−2 if trained).
Crit rangesCrit hit: 3–4 always · 5 if skill ≥15 · 6 if skill ≥16. Crit miss: 18 always · 17 if skill ≤15. Full table →

CRIT Critical Success & Failure

Crit success: nat 3–4 always; nat 5 if skill ≥ 15; nat 6 if skill ≥ 16.  Crit failure: nat 18 always; nat 17 if skill ≤ 15; margin of failure ≥ 10.
Defence crit: auto-defend + attacker is off-balance (−2 DX, defences & skills until they Ready). Crit Parry = free Aggressive Parry to attacking limb; Crit Dodge +1 m free step; Crit Block drives attacker back. Crit fail (def): auto-fail + attacker may roll Crit Hit.
Skill/HT/Will/IQ crit: success = ideal/quick resist; fail = worst/lingering (2× FP on Force SW).

HOUSE Crit Hit → Counter-Crit Defence. On a critical hit the defender MAY still attempt an active defence, but it succeeds ONLY on a critical defence of its own (nat 3–4; 5 at def ≥ 15; 6 at def ≥ 16) — else the hit lands. DR still applies; damage rolled normally then modified per the row. Extra defences take the multi-def penalty & each needs its own crit. Lightsaber Parry SW vs a critical bolt follows the same rule.

HOUSE Turn Economy — Quick Action & Special Reaction

Your turn = ONE maneuver · a free active defence vs each attack you can perceive (out of turn — no maneuver cost) · any number of Free Actions · 1 Quick Action · 1 Special Reaction (Force). Quick Action rides alongside your maneuver: Force Push/Pull (full FP), Fast-Draw, or a single-touch device activation — defensive QA is forbidden during All-Out Attack, offensive QA & Fast-Draw stay legal. Special Reaction (Force-only, full FP) is a triggered Force Push/Pull layered on an active defence and does not spend the defence slot — defensive use (redirect a threat) is forbidden during AoA, offensive use (shove/pull a foe) is legal.

RES Damage Resolution — Step by Step

Roll basic damage from the weapon (e.g. 2d+1). This number drives both injury and knockback.
Subtract armour: penetration = damage − (DR ÷ Armour Divisor). If DR ≥ that, nothing gets through (see Blunt Trauma). AD scales the armour, not the roll: standard AD 1 = full DR · heavy blaster / military AD 2 = half DR · ion AD 5 (anti-droid) · disruptor AD 10 · lightsaber AD ∞ = ignores DR (vs non-cortosis).
Apply wound multiplier to penetration by damage type & location (see Damage Types): injury = penetration × mult. Vitals ×3, Skull ×4, etc.
Resolve location effects: crippling, major wound (> HP/2 → HT vs stun + knockdown), bleeding / cauterisation, knockback (below & in Hit Location).
Subtract injury from HP and apply shock, then check Reeling / Consciousness / Death as thresholds are crossed.

Worked: bolt rolls 8, target DR 6, weapon AD 2 → eff DR 3 → pen 5 → tib-burn ×1 = 5 HP. To Vitals: ×3 = 15 HP (major wound).

Special Damage Outcomes
KnockbackCr (full) & cut (half) only; not imp/pi. 1 m back per full multiple of (target ST−2) in basic damage (before wound mult), whether or not it penetrates DR. Knocked 2+ m → roll best of DX/Acrobatics/Judo or fall prone (−1/m beyond 1st). Into a wall: 1 HP cr per metre not travelled.
Blunt TraumaFlexible armour (vest, mesh, leather) that stops a hit still transmits 1 HP crushing per 5 pts stopped (round down), ignoring DR. Rigid armour (plate, ceramic) does not. Not from energy/burn.
BleedingTriggers: NeckV/A, or any cut > ⅓ HP to a limb. Each turn HT or lose 1 HP; First Aid roll stops it. Lasts the engagement if untreated.
Cauterisation SWLightsaber wounds: full dmg & all effects but no bleeding; any sever/cripple is permanent (Force-heal/prosthetic only). Blaster bolts bleed at half rate; slugthrowers bleed normally.

KB Knockback & Heavy Impact

KnockbackPer full (ST − 2) of a blow’s basic damage (before the wound multiplier), the target is shoved 1 hex. Crushing = full · cutting = half · impaling/piercing = none (they punch through). Happens even if DR stops the wound.
Falling downShoved 1 hex = stay up. 2+ hexes → roll best of DX / Acrobatics / Judo or fall prone (−1 per hex past the first).
Into a wallSlammed into a hard object → +1 HP crushing per metre not travelled (the stop you didn’t get to make).
Heavy Impact HOUSECannon-class weapons (assault/blaster cannons, rockets) knock back like crushing even when they burn and even when DR stops the bolt. Rigid plate that stops one still passes 1 HP crushing per full 10 stopped (concussion through plate). Beskar stops the bolt — not the shove.

REACT Reactions — Everything You Can Do on Someone Else’s Turn

These happen out of turn, triggered by an attack, at no maneuver cost. First your active defence — one per attack you can perceive (none vs a rear / surprise hit); each extra defence that turn is −4 cumulative (−2 with a Trained-by-a-Master / Weapon-Master skill). You also get 1 Special Reaction (Force) that does not spend the defence slot.

GUARD Defending for an Ally & the Thrown Saber

To defend another body you must be able to reach the attack — with your own body, your blade, or the Force. You can’t cover someone across the room.

Body interpose (anyone)Sacrificial Dodge: dive in front of an adjacent ally (1 hex). Dodge −2 — success, the attack misses; failure, you take it instead. You end prone. Works vs melee or a bolt (incl. screening a downed ally from a crit). Not from prone; not after AoA / Committed / Move&Attack. Uses a defence (multi-def after).
Blade / shield interpose HOUSENeeds a Protective stance (Soresu & guardian arts): as your Reaction, Block or Parry an attack aimed at a chosen ally within your weapon’s reach (saber reach 1–2 hex), +3, interposing as a free action 1/turn. Without that stance a normal Parry/Block guards only you — for an ally you must body-block (above).
Force redirect SWSpecial Reaction (Push/Pull, 1/turn, does not use your defence slot): shove a threat off-line, or shove self or an adjacent ally clear. QC vs attacker skill. Forbidden during AoA / AoD.
The Thrown Saber — Recall & the Guardian Throw
Saber Throw (RAW)An Attack (your maneuver), not a Reaction — and while the blade is in flight you cannot Parry. FP recall is built in: 4 FP → returns at the start of your next turn · 6 FP → snaps back the same turn at end of your action. A Force-sensitive target may catch it (QC) → return delayed 1 turn.
Guardian Throw HOUSEYou may hurl the lit blade as a Reaction to guard an ally in throw range (10 hex) who is attacked — melee or bolt. It spends your Reaction (your free defence that turn) + the Saber Throw FP (4 / 6 above). Roll Lightsaber Parry −4: success defends the ally just as if you’d parried for them (bolt → Deflect rules; melee → blocked). You are disarmed — no saber Parry for yourself — until the blade returns. Cannot interpose vs an area attack.

AOE Explosions & Area of Effect

The falloff rule. An explosion deals its full listed damage at the centre hex, then weakens as it spreads: damage = centre ÷ (3 × hexes away). So 1 hex out is ÷3, 2 hexes ÷6, 3 hexes ÷9. The table below is pre-divided — just read across to the victim’s hex.

Then armour. Apply the weapon’s armour divisor & the victim’s DR after falloff. Concussion is cr (plate resists); frag & shrapnel are pi (ignore shields); plasma/thermal is burn (energy). SW

BLAST Surviving Area Attacks — Grenades & the Instant Detonator

You defend a blast only if you see it coming, and no Parry, Block or saber Deflect works against an area attack — your outs are Dodge, Dive for Cover, hard cover and the Force. Each defender in the radius rolls their own.

One Dodge = one ringA normal active Dodge steps you 1 hex — that drops you one column on the falloff table (centre → ÷3). Against a thermal det (≈63 at centre → ≈21 one hex out) a single step is rarely enough to live.
Dive for Cover HOUSEThe real answer. An All-Out-Defence (Dodge) you may take as your Reaction to the throw: roll Dodge or Acrobatics and cross several hexes toward cover in one motion, ending prone. Each hex crossed steps you down another column; reaching cover then subtracts its DR; Acrobatic Dodge stacks (+2).
Cover beats everythingCover DR comes off after falloff — a wall, a corner, a body between you and centre. Diving prone in your own hex doesn’t cut the damage but can put cover in the way.
Timing is the killerEnd-of-turn nades (frag, concussion, plasma) let foes walk clear on their own turn before the bang — and can be thrown back with a Ready. Instant nades (thermal detonator — blows on landing) give no turn to flee and no throw-back window: your only out is the Reaction Dodge / Dive in that instant. That is why a thermal det “guts a room.”
A near-miss still stunsEven a non-wounding blast: HT vs the centre damage or be stunned (lose your next turn). Sealed armour negates gas; nothing negates concussion.

What FP isYour stamina pool. Spent on Extra Effort, hard physical skills (swim, climb), holding your breath, and — for Force-users — every Force power. Stun bolts and fatigue damage drain FP, not HP.

HVY Parrying Heavy Hits & the Strength to Hold

A parry stops a blow by meeting its force. Meet something far heavier than your guard and the guard is driven aside — blade, arm and all.

Heavy melee weapons
Parry-heavyParrying a two-handed weapon, or one swung with ST 3+ above your weapon’s, with a light blade → roll DX (or weapon skill) to keep the parry, or it’s beaten aside. Fencing parries suffer most.
Overpower a guardThe attacker can spend muscle to break your defence — a Quick Contest of ST (see BASH). Win and your block weakens or fails.
Lightsaber vs blaster bolts — deflect, redirect, reflect
Deflect (default)A lit-saber Parry at full skill turns any ordinary bolt — pistol to heavy blaster — to a random hex, no ST roll. A critical parry reflects it back for free. (Cannon-class fire, below, is the exception.)
Redirect (Parry −2)Steer it to a hex you choose — a wall, the floor, clear of an ally — not a random one. Just lands there; no new attack.
Reflect — back at them (Parry −4)The bolt becomes your shot at the original shooter: re-roll it on your Lightsaber Parry skill (not theirs), at your range back to them. They defend normally but seldom expect their own bolt, so it lands often. Each parry is separate — a full volley hits the multi-defence cap (~5–6/turn).
Cannon-class boltsAssault / blaster cannons, rockets — the Heavy Impact bracket — hit the blade like a battering ram. To hold the Deflect, roll ST at −1 per full 5 points of the bolt’s basic damage above your ST. (Average the dice: a 6d cannon ≈ 21.)
Hold (success)The bolt is turned — but the impact shoves you back 1 hex (more on a big margin).
Beaten aside (fail)The bolt drives through: it strikes you (with Heavy Impact knockback even if DR stops the wound), and roll DX or the saber is knocked from your grip.
Too big to muscleTurbolaser-scale fire can’t be held. Dodge, take cover, or bleed it with the Force.
