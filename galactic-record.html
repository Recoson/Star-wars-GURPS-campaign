Part One

The Galactic Chronicle

The history of the galaxy as it is recorded and remembered — from the ancient wars that scarred a thousand worlds, through the rise and fall of Revan, to the fragile present. This is the public account: what scholars teach and senators cite. What it does not contain is set down at its end, among the unexplained.

c. 3996 BBY · The Old Sith WarsThe Age of Ancient Darkness

Before living memory, the galaxy was twice nearly unmade. The first cataclysm came when the fallen Jedi Exar Kun and Ulic Qel-Droma raised the banner of the resurrected Sith and plunged the Republic into the Great Sith War. They drew to their cause the dark cultists of the Krath, who seized the Empress Teta system, and the war-clans of the Mandalorians, who relished any conflict worth the dying. From the Deep Core to the Outer Rim, worlds burned.

The Jedi and the Republic broke them at terrible cost, and from that victory the galaxy inherited the conviction it still holds: that the Sith are extinct — a barbarism overcome, a closed chapter. The dead were entombed and the tombs were sealed. Yet some graves never quieted. The spirit of the ancient Sith king Freedon Nadd still broods beneath the jungles of Dxun, the moon of Onderon, a reminder that the dark does not always stay buried where it is put.

c. 3976–3960 BBYThe Mandalorian Wars

Generations later the Mandalorians returned, no longer a war-clan but a crusade. Under Mandalore the Ultimate, the Neo-Crusaders swept the Outer Rim, world after world falling while the cautious Jedi Council counselled patience. Two young Jedi refused that patience: the strategist Revan and his closest friend Malak. Against the Council's will they rallied a generation of knights and the broken Republic fleets, and they turned the war.

The victory was bought with horror. At Malachor V, the superweapon called the Mass Shadow Generator was triggered, annihilating both fleets at once and crushing the world into a graveyard of gravity and bone. The Jedi general who gave that order — remembered afterward only as the Exile — did the unthinkable to survive the death of so many: she severed herself from the Force entirely. The Mandalorians were shattered as a power, their mask-helm taken; Revan and Malak vanished into the Unknown Regions, chasing something they would not name.

c. 3959–3956 BBY · KOTORThe Jedi Civil War

Revan and Malak did not die in the Rim — they fell. They returned as Dark Lords of the Sith, having found an ancient engine of war: the Star Forge, a factory-leviathan of the long-dead Rakata that fed on the dark side and a nearby star to birth endless fleets. With it they brought the exhausted Republic to the edge of ruin.

Then Malak betrayed his master, firing on Revan's own ship to claim the title of Dark Lord. Revan survived, mind shattered. The Jedi Council took the broken conqueror, rebuilt a false identity, and — not knowing what they did — set Revan loose to hunt the Star Forge as their unwitting agent. Across Taris, Dantooine, Tatooine, Kashyyyk, Manaan, and the Sith tombs of Korriban, Revan gathered the fragments of the Star Maps that led to the Forge. The truth surfaced aboard Malak's flagship, the Leviathan; the memory returned; and at the Forge itself, above the Rakatan homeworld of Lehon, Revan struck Malak down and ended the war.

Afterward, Revan once more walked away — alone, into the Unknown Regions, driven by a prophecy of a great and gathering threat beyond the galaxy's edge that no one else could see and that even Revan could not fully name. Revan has not returned. To the galaxy, the Prodigal Knight is now legend, argued over in cantinas and classrooms alike — and the warning is dismissed as the obsession of a broken mind.

c. 3951 BBY · KOTOR IIThe First Jedi Purge & the Dark Wars

The peace did not hold. In the war's shadow a new darkness hunted the survivors — not an army, but three predators who called themselves the Sith Triumvirate. Darth Nihilus was a hunger given form, a void that devoured the life of whole worlds and the Force itself; Darth Sion, the Lord of Pain, was a corpse held together by nothing but hatred; and behind them moved their teacher, Darth Traya — once the Jedi Kreia. One by one the scattered Jedi were murdered, until the Order was all but erased and the few survivors went into hiding or death.

Into this emptiness returned the Exile — Meetra Surik, the general of Malachor, the last Jedi to walk the galaxy openly. From the mining wreck of Peragus to the restoration world of Telos IV, she rebuilt her connection to the Force — and, unlike the Order of old, she did not walk alone. The companions who would become legends journeyed with her throughout, a true band rather than a string of strangers: the rogue Atton Rand, the Miraluka seer Visas Marr, the Zabrak tech-savant Bao-Dur, the bounty huntress Mira, the scholar Mical, the Echani Brianna, and the blind, manipulative old woman Kreia whose counsel hid a Dark Lord. Together they crossed Nar Shaddaa, Dxun and Onderon, Dantooine, and at last Malachor V itself, where the Triumvirate fell.

Among those Meetra sought out was an old hero of the Mandalorian Wars: Tylo Dara. By then the estranged Iktotchi had become a fugitive of the Force — roaming the stars alone, using his gifts to narrowly evade the Triumvirate's hunters who culled every sensitive they found. Meetra found him, and from that moment he fought at her side through all of it. But the war's end demanded the same terrible price of him that it had once demanded of her: the agony of returning to Malachor V was so total that Tylo was forced to forgo his powers and leave his lightsaber behind in the dark — and there, in the ruined academy, the lingering presence of Kreia's ghost remained, waiting. It was on that same blood-soaked ground that Tylo crossed blades with the Mandalorian Sekan Nightfall — a lone, clanless warrior who did not yet know that Canderous had even survived the Dark Wars, let alone that he would take up the mask. They fought to a perfect draw — and by Mandalorian honor, Sekan was left holding a combat blood debt: one day, when Tylo stands again at his full power, the two must duel to the death. Until that day, Sekan follows him — guarding him, keeping him alive, waiting for the rematch fate owes them both. Meanwhile the veteran Canderous Ordo took up the ancient mask and became Mandalore — but at first in seclusion. Only after the war's end did he begin the slow, quiet work of calling the broken clans home, and for a long while scattered warriors like Sekan had no idea their people were being gathered again at all.

c. 3951–3942 BBY · SWTOR-Era FoundationsThe Restoration

The years since the Triumvirate have been an uneasy mending — and the seeds of everything now in motion. A weary Galactic Republic, all but bankrupt and deep in debt to the banking clans, poured its remaining strength into the Restoration Zones of Telos and Dantooine, rebuilding what the wars had gutted. Meetra Surik refounded the Jedi Order — smaller, harder, and far less dogmatic than the Council of old — drawing scattered survivors and newfound sensitives toward the ice world of Ilum and, in time, to Telos and Coruscant.

Beyond the Republic's tired heart, new powers gathered. The reunified Mandalorian clans passed from Canderous toward a new generation. The cold, isolationist Chiss Ascendancy of the Unknown Regions, long a rumor, began at last to make formal contact. And in the streets and senates a louder voice rose — the Silent Hand, an anti-Force populist movement preaching that the galaxy would never know peace until both Jedi and Sith were spent like coin. Czerka, Adascorp, the banking clans, and the great syndicates grew fat in the gaps the wars had left.

c. 3941 BBY · The Present AgeThe Csilla Accords & the New Map

The present age is defined by a single event: the great inter-faction Summit on Csilla, hosted by the Chiss in their frozen Unknown-Regions capital, where the powers of the galaxy gathered to redraw the order of things. It was nearly drowned in blood — a hidden conspiracy struck at the summit with an army of perfect impostors (see Part II) — but it survived, and from it came the accords that shape the galaxy as it now stands.

c. 3941 BBY · The Accords

From the Csilla Summit: the Jedi Order is formally restored under Republic charter and granted the world of Tython, its ancient cradle. The Mandalorians regain Mandalore itself and the right to militarize under Republic oversight, and are bound as galactic first-responders. The Chiss take wardenship of the Unknown Regions but decline all alliance and trade until they have studied the wider galaxy. The Unexplained — What the Chronicle Omits

Rumor, anomaly, and absence. No authority claims these. They are recorded so they are not forgotten.
  • Worlds going dark. Settled systems toward the rim fall silent, then seem to vanish from the relays altogether. Investments and populations quietly withdraw.
  • Ships that do not return. Vessels venturing into certain reaches of the Unknown Regions are lost at rates no storm or pirate explains; the lanes are being abandoned.
  • The unknown warship. A vessel of a make no fleet recognizes — vast and heavily armed — has appeared at the edge of an engagement before vanishing into hyperspace.
  • The waking machines. Ancient Rakatan technology, long thought dead, has been found stirring on more than one world. Corporate and Republic interests race quietly to understand it — and to find the lost Foundry that Revan is said to have sought.
  • The impostors. The Csilla crisis revealed living infiltrators able to wear other faces perfectly. Who made them, and on whose order, remains officially unanswered.

The Crew's Trail

The campaign as it has unfolded — the long road of the crew of the Dara Dara, from a quiet posting on Alderaan to the frozen halls of Csilla and the buried vaults of the deep rim — roughly 3943–3940 BBY. Told in order, world by world, as the players have lived it.

Chapter I · c. 3943 BBY · Alderaan

The Iktotchi in the Dark

House Thul · the casino · the train · the cleared house

For years Chatni served the Alderaanian noblewoman Cardina Thul under the name Nerry — a quiet, useful operative in a quiet, useful life. That ended when a horned stranger interrupted her breakfast: Tylo Dara, an Iktotchi ex-Jedi master who had been hunting her for half a year on the strength of his premonitions, trailed by the Mandalorian Sekan Nightfall, who would not let an unfinished duel with Tylo go. Cardina set them all on a single mission — the discovery that Iktotchi were being held and tortured somewhere in the house's reach.

Tylo played bait and was duly snatched from a casino and nearly frozen in carbonite; Chatni shadowed, the team struck, and a running fight through a train and a comms-dead hangar followed. Sekan soaked fire like only a Mandalorian can while Tylo raced to open the hangar doors. They won, then were sent straight into a second house-cleaning of "bad, bad people" — Sekan punched Tylo mid-ambush, Tylo nearly executed the surrendering ringleader, and Chatni swore (twice) never to work with the pair again. Cardina, delighted, paid well and hired them all again anyway. The Iktotchi thread — and Tylo's vow to free his people — had been opened.

Chapter II · c. 3943 BBY · The Deep Expedition

Six Months Beneath the Sea

a hidden world · the underwater Sith bases · Jera · the Jedi temple

Cardina sent the crew — now joined by the blind, dangerous DeLuca — on a months-long expedition to recover secrets House Thul wanted buried. They found ships dragged down by a strange seismic event, a fortune in credits to be laundered (which Chatni promptly mined with a bomb), and, beneath the waves, a chain of dead Sith bunkers: a Sith woman's helmet, lightsaber scars on the walls, and submarines pointing deeper still. Two months of self-taught submarining later they reached a second base — and were met by the rogue dark-sider Jera.

Tylo charmed a truce: the crew would fetch what Jera wanted from a third, flooding base in exchange for the location of a Jedi temple — and a hint of an artifact of Chatni's own people. They delivered, skimming rare lightsaber metals on the way out, and Jera gave Chatni a bowl as a "gift" and an invitation to the gathering on Ilum. At the temple, only DeLuca could open the door; inside lay his late mother's crystal, holocrons, and a log of all who had come before. Then it went wrong: Chatni amplified DeLuca's meditation on his mother, both rolled brilliantly and catastrophically, and DeLuca's mind flared across the entire galaxy — lighting up hundreds of Force-sensitives for the Silent Hand to find, and brushing the deep-space presence of his own father. The Silent Hand's ships were already inbound; Chatni was nearly killed by orbital fire. Tylo and Sekan destroyed three ships to drag her out, and she spent weeks in a kolto tank. The ship was repainted and renamed the Dara Dara.

Chapter III · c. 3942 BBY · Dxun

The Mandalore and the Tomb

the jungle moon · Canderous · Freedon Nadd · Alra Wren

On the Mandalore-held moon of Dxun the crew met Alra Wren — a spirited Mandalorian artificer Chatni adored on sight — and the aging Canderous Ordo, still bearing the mask of Mandalore. For Sekan this was a revelation: only here did he learn that Canderous had survived the Dark Wars at all, had taken up the mask, and had quietly drawn the scattered clans back together — and that this reborn Mandalorian power now stood as an ally to the very crew he travelled with. Sekan fought for the honor of his line and won, becoming a chieftain. While hunting a lost Mandalorian the crew battled a dark-side beast, and Chatni's spirit sight located the tomb of the ancient Sith king Freedon Nadd — its growing influence a slow poison on the moon. The crew secured Alra's company (a six-month loan), drank deep, and set course for Ilum by way of a lightsaber rumored to lie on the dead world of Malachor V.

Chapter IV · c. 3942 BBY · Malachor V

The Grey Blade Reclaimed

the shattered world · the Sith academy · the abandoned saber · Kreia's ghost

The "lost" lightsaber the crew sought on the gravity-wracked corpse of Malachor was no stranger's — it was Tylo's own, the blade he had cast aside in the ruined Sith academy years before, when the agony of this place at the end of the Dark Wars forced him to renounce his powers. He had come back to take it up again. But Malachor does not give its dead back freely: the lingering ghost of Kreia — Darth Traya still haunted the academy, and as Tylo's hand closed on the hilt the spirit-echo nearly killed him. He survived transformed — no longer simply a Jedi, but grey, his power returned and changed all at once. Chatni picked the academy's automated door; they glimpsed a great beast; and they fled as the unstable world tore itself further apart, making at last for Ilum.

Chapter V · c. 3942 BBY · Ilum

The Gathering of the Force

the ice caves · Meetra Surik · the bowl · Master Voll · the crystal

Ilum was where the scattered Force survivors had gathered around Meetra Surik, the Exile herself, alongside Brianna, Mira, the rogue dark-sider Jera, and the hardliner Master Voll — who had once tried to blow Chatni's head apart. Chatni and the Exile took each other's measure; Meetra learned Chatni's true name and saw the Nightsister beneath the alias. Tylo dueled and beat Jera. The crew handed DeLuca's care to Meetra.

The cursed bowl finally revealed itself as a dark-side trap; Meetra, furious, forced Jera to help undo it and to sever the Force from the murderous Master Voll. Chatni built a flawless gas trap to capture Voll; Jera, with Chatni's new grasp of Sith alchemy, ripped his connection away. Jera then drained the bowl's power for herself and produced a small, sinister ally — Lord Ratek — to Meetra's fury. Amid it all, the crew learned a kyber crystal meant for Chatni waited on Ilum; they excavated it from a cave-beast's lair. Meetra set two missions: the resort-world Makeb, and the rescue of a man held aboard a Silent Hand leviathan. Alra began teaching Chatni to build a lightsaber.

Chapter VI · c. 3942 BBY · Makeb

The Shan and the Zabrak

the floating mesas · Satele Shan · Kao Cen Darach

On the lush resort-world of Makeb, Chatni and Tylo together won over two extraordinary allies who had stood apart from the reborn Order — and brought them into it. The first was Satele Shan, in this telling the daughter of Revan and Bastila Shan, heir to the two greatest legends of the age. The second was the Zabrak master Kao Cen Darach — a warrior-philosopher who had never been one of Meetra's KOTOR-era survivors, and who joined the Jedi only because the crew of the Dara Dara sought him out and earned his trust. He would become one of Chatni's truest teachers. On the long voyage home Chatni learned a lightsaber stance from Kao Cen and practiced battle meditation with Tylo, while she and Alra stole a perfect afternoon in Makeb's lakes and waterfalls.

Chapter VII · c. 3942 BBY · The Leviathan Strike

Twenty-Six Thousand

a Silent Hand warship · the rescue · the bomb

Back on Ilum, Chatni refused Meetra's "gorilla" assault in favor of her own signature: a teleport-door breach and a 10-kilogram bomb, funded personally by the Exile at 400,000 credits. Tylo flew them in under stolen camo-crystals and set them beside the prisoner's cell; Chatni cracked the force-field under fire (rolling brilliantly), they pulled the man free through her portal — he snatched an artifact on the way — and she detonated. The warship died, and twenty-six thousand with it. Chatni felt every one of them. She is, by her own grim accounting, a committer of war crimes now.

Chapter VIII · c. 3942 BBY · The Matriarch's Mark

Spirit Sever

the Ilum wilds · the Nightsister mothers

Meditating alone on her "slight genocide," Chatni was ambushed in her own mind by the old Nightsister matriarchs of Dathomir. She killed the previous mother and forced the rest back, waking exhausted and covered in the burning tattoos of the matriarch's sigil — and possessing a new and terrible power: spirit sever. The mark of a matriarchal candidate had surfaced; her heritage would not leave her alone.

Chapter IX · c. 3941 BBY · Dxun & Tatooine

The Succession and the Zombies

Canderous fading · Torren Wren · the Rakghoul desert · Luxa

Returning to Dxun, the crew found morale in ruins: the Freedon Nadd tomb had grown stronger and the aging Canderous, never having left the moon, was being slowly bent by it. Chatni gently healed his mind enough that he could be persuaded to step down, naming the steady Torren Wren as the new Mandalore. They took the recovering Canderous with them to Tatooine to buy ships for Meetra's struggling people.

Tatooine was a Czerka misery of queues and quarantine. The crew dug out four buried ships, took three, and answered an SOS from a mercenary band and their Cathar archaeologist client — finding them besieged by Czerka workers turned to fast, mindless infected by some plague. The crew cleared them out; Alra was nearly killed and saved only by a rare off-world surgeon. To pay for it, Chatni had to face the Exchange's Luxa — a slick, pink-skinned operator who extorted 200,000 credits and a great deal of smug. The crew slipped away with Alra alive, all four flying out in separate ships, Chatni and Alra bonding over the long first night.

Chapter X · c. 3941 BBY · Kashyyyk

The Wookiee Exodus and Revan's Allies

the Mandalorian relocation · battle meditation · the Rakatan archive

The Mandalorians had abandoned Dxun for Kashyyyk, negotiating with the Wookiee chieftain for a foothold. Chatni offered Torren her battle meditation; he answered that it was "not a deal but mutual trust," and let her enhance his hunting parties through her brother-in-arms Kultak. During one hunt Tylo pressed the wrong control in an ancient structure and vanished — teleported to a hidden Rakatan archive, where a triangle-headed being interrogated him. Tylo bluffed his way through by invoking Revan (who had allied with these beings) and the password of Satele Shan, daughter of Bastila. In return he was given a holo cataloguing hundreds of Jedi held in stasis on a distant world, and intelligence on the system where the Chiss and the "Open Palm" met. Through this, the crew first learned of a Mandalorian tracker named Brunos (to whom Chatni accidentally bonded), and the new chieftain Drax.

Chapter XI · c. 3941 BBY · The Citadel

GOTO, the Genoharadan, and a Name Revealed

the Mandalorian summit · the Exile · the secret meeting

Aboard a Mandalorian warship the crew reached the Citadel for a great pre-Summit council. There the shape of the galaxy's near future was drawn: the Mandalorians gained standing; the Jedi Order regained its privileges under the Republic and the world of Python (Tython); the Iktotchi were named arbiters of disputes; and Cardina Thul took the Jedi enclave onto her own world. In a smaller, sealed meeting, the crime-lord intelligence GOTO exposed Chatni utterly — her true name, her Black Cell past, her old contract with HK-47 — and revealed the true power behind her troubles: the Genoharadan, a tiny cabal of immensely connected people who had marked her since she killed a senator on Korriban. Senator Cressa set the crew a brutal job: identify the Genoharadan's members and tag their ships at the coming Summit so they could be ripped from hyperspace and boarded. Payment: a planet. Chatni accepted. She also learned that the Exchange's Luxa had risen to Second Hand, that Tylo and the Iktotchi General Kladar now shared a premonition of a vast coming war, and that Sekan had been quietly told by Torren to keep watch on the crew and report back.

Chapter XII · c. 3941 BBY · The Genoharadan Hunt

From Herdessa to the Kabaal

Mon Gazza · the Hoth Czerka station · the Valia kidnapping · the summit massacre · the Hunter

The hunt ranged across the galaxy. On Herdessa, GOTO confirmed he wanted the Genoharadan dead too, and pointed the way. Atop a spire, Chatni interrogated the honest Senator Mon Gazza, learning of Senator Valia — Quarren security chief and banking-guild power — and the demilitarization scheme called Scalpel. At a Czerka station near Hoth, Chatni teleported through the archives, killed a Force-sensitive Black Cell agent, and learned the worst of it: Czerka was researching Rakatan technology, hunting the Foundry and a record left by Revan, and tracking something for fourteen years. As the crew fled, a vast, unknown warship none of them recognized tore out of hyperspace and opened fire.

With Atton Rand hired on, Chatni kidnapped Senator Valia from her own Coruscant office (disguised by the skin-grafter Vessa), severed her from the Force, and broke loose the Genoharadan's secrets: fourteen members who do not know each other's names; a goal of "stability and protection from the outer rim"; a meeting-place called the Kabaal, a refitted, invisible Imperial-era station that opens only to the Black Codex; and a chilling note — that some of the fourteen might be one person in five bodies. At the Kabaal, Chatni laid traps and waited as five of the six arrived: the envoy Kovacs in Revan-era Sith armor; the Hunter, blond and shielded; the "shaper" Director Polard; a glasses-fused Arkanian scientist; and an old "prince." She blew them apart — but the Hunter did not even flinch, executed the wounded Polard ("for the Republic"), and proved, when Atton finally took his head, to be no man at all: sparks, not blood. The crew escaped the collapsing station with the Genoharadan broken but the Hunter's true nature — and the lost Codex — unresolved.

Chapter XIII · c. 3941 BBY · Manaan

The Day of Peace

the Selkath ocean-world · Atton

Atton took Chatni to Manaan, the Selkath ocean-world and the galaxy's source of kolto, where he was known and welcomed — the door always open at old Morsha's family store, the closest thing Atton keeps to a home — and she was gifted a ring marking her as a guest of the Selkath. For one day there was no war: extraordinary food, a submarine ride, an underwater meditation in which Chatni reached enlightenment — and Atton held her hand. It was, she realized, the only true peace she had felt. He finished teaching her the deceptive Trakata saber technique on the way home.

Chapter XIV · c. 3941 BBY · Iktotch

Tylo's Honors and the Nightsister Prophecy

the Iktotchi homeworld · the new Order · Chatni's heritage

On Tylo's homeworld moon of Iktotch, the precognitive Iktotchi welcomed him as a hero, heaped him with honors and titles, and — at his charismatic urging — granted the founding of a new Order. They tested Chatni's heritage through their visions and the seers of the Ember Conclave: she learned she is a matriarchal candidate of Dathomir, that a dark prophecy holds she could "push the galaxy into darkness" if she united the Nightsisters, and the deep history of her people — that the Kwa first gave Dathomir and Iktotch the Force and the stars, that the Rakata then enslaved and the Sith later slaughtered, and that a banished Jedi had seeded Dathomir with the Nightsisters a thousand years gone. Chatni and Tylo would be raised as statues on Iktotch. General Kladar, Tylo's old war-friend and a commander of the Stormguard, faced an arranged marriage; Tylo would be his best man.

Chapter XV · c. 3941 BBY · Alderaan, Again

Queen of the Mountain

Cardina ascendant · the Jedi enclave · Bao-Dur, Mira, Satele

Returning to Alderaan, the crew found Cardina Thul transformed — crowned, royal, having "gutted" rival houses to consolidate power, and hosting the restored Jedi enclave on her world. There Chatni trained: teaching from Mira, personal-shield work from the explosively cheerful Bao-Dur, and quiet hours with Satele Shan, whose focus had begun to drift toward something stirring in the Unknown Regions. They also glimpsed a perfect double of the Hunter on Alderaan — proof of cloning. Cardina, now a monarch, made plain that Chatni would one day have to choose a side.

Chapter XVI · c. 3941 BBY · Tython

The Crossroads

the Jedi cradle · the path decisions · severing Dathomir

On Tython, the ancient Jedi homeworld now granted to the Order, Chatni weighed her future against the counsel of Meetra, the scholar Mical, and the Zabrak master Kao Cen Darach. Her bonds, her tattoos, and the well of dark power on Dathomir were all chains. She ended her master-apprentice tie to Tylo, and resolved on the hardest road: to sever herself from Dathomir entirely. The act nearly killed her and shattered her lightsaber, but Canderous — now an addict for the life she had given him, and oddly devoted — stood guard over her body until it was done.

Chapter XVII · c. 3941 BBY · Csilla

The Summit and the Impostors

the Chiss homeworld · the Iktotchi arbiters · the doppelgänger crisis

The great Summit on Csilla convened in the Chiss capital — an ice world of rigid blood-hierarchy, ringed by Chiss warships and stationed Silent Hand vessels, security so total that lightsabers were catalogued at the door. Here the Iktotchi sat as the summit's neutral arbiters, and every great power of the galaxy gathered in one place: Republic, Jedi, Mandalorians, Chiss, the corporations and banking guilds, Cardina's Alderaan, the Selkath, and the Exchange's ever-present Luxa. Chatni worked the security levels, mapping patrols and tagging the Genoharadan ships for Cressa's trap.

Then the killing began. Sekan spotted a doppelgänger; Chatni confirmed a guard was a shapeshifter; and the body count climbed by the hour as perfect impostors murdered their targets and took their places. The crew raced to gather the people who mattered — Cardina, Mon Gazza, the Iktotchi delegation, Chancellor Cressa, the Selkath — onto Meetra's ship before the slaughter could claim them, and broke a captured shifter's mind. The truth: the Genoharadan (the Hunter's work) had grown an army of clones — thirty-five already in place, seventy more to come, thousands off-world — with a kill-priority list led by GOTO, the Jedi, and Cardina. Cardina announced the conspiracy from the summit floor; the building turned into a level-by-level war as the impostors grew Force-weapons from their own flesh. In the chaos a Chiss officer cornered Chatni and said, quietly: "I would appreciate it if you'd stop tracking me across the galaxy." The crisis was survived, the Exchange was named the Chiss' first official enemy, GOTO and Luxa walked away in cuffs (and out again), and the Hunter was reported dead — his fortune, and his Codex, lost. From the wreckage came the Csilla Accords that now order the galaxy.

Chapter XVIII · c. 3941 BBY · Ord Cestus

The Reckoning with Jera

the echo of Dathomir · battle meditation · the Book of Shadows

Suspecting the dark-sider Jera of meddling with Dathomir, the crew lured her into a duel. From her ship Chatni wove her finest battle meditation, painting a chamber floor-to-ceiling in her own blood as a ritual focus, while Tylo, Sekan, and a now-thriving Canderous fought Jera, her pet Sith Lord Ratek, and a channeling Nightsister. They won without a scratch. Struck down, Jera spat a dying curse and an omen — "When they return, you will be the first to die" — and surrendered her ghost. The crew took her ship, her saber, and a copy of the Book of Shadows; Canderous, fully alive and now Chatni's strange devotee, declared her his friend.

Chapter XIX · c. 3940 BBY · Tython, Again

The White Blade

the meditations · the lightsaber reborn · holocrons · Atton

Back on Tython, Chatni at last walked her own road. Two weeks of meditation in the light-side springs offered her three paths — communion, empowerment, or binding — and she chose herself: skill, knowledge, and a deeper understanding of the Force and its language, working with the Force rather than chaining it. She rebuilt her shattered weapon from the broken pieces and the ancient saber-parts she had gathered — a blade with three states, blue-eyed and pure white, that had waited thousands of years for a wielder who needed it rather than wanted it. She mastered the creation of holocrons and a portal-network few living have managed. And in the small hours, as he packed his ship to slip away, Atton Rand kissed her and told her the new her suited her well.

Chapter XX · c. 3940 BBY · Bel Savas

The Buried Vault

the artificial ice-world · Soah the Infernal One · the imprisoned races · the descent

Seeking materials for her holocrons, Chatni and Tylo (with Satele) made for Bel Savas, an ice world whose artificial ecosystems concealed a colossal Rakatan vault. Inside they found a handful of escaped slaves of a race called the Gormak, hundreds of thousands more in stasis pods, and — at the bottom — the last living Rakatan emperor, Soah the Infernal One, imprisoned by his own people for experimenting on his own kind, and quietly insane after millennia of forced dreaming. He knew Revan; he held fifty-six imprisoned races and millions of individuals; and he set the crew a devil's bargain to free them.

What followed went deep into places the public Chronicle does not record: a dream-walking guide from the crystal-skinned people of Voss, glimpses of an ancient spy named Xesh, and a final confrontation with imprisoned dark spirits older than the Sith Wars — beings that offered Chatni knowledge, power, and a throne, and whom she refused and destroyed. At the last she stood again at her crossroads and chose, as she always has, her own path. Chapter XXI · c. 3940 BBY · Ord Cestus

The Rival and the End of Jera

On Ord Cestus the sisterhood reached for Chatni in the deep places of her mind — and beside the visions stood the rival candidate, swollen with the stolen power of six dead candidates and shepherded by two Nightmothers. With them was Jera, now openly drawing on Dathomir's dark well. Goaded past sense by Tylo, Jera came to fight; the crew met her, her tame Sith, and a coven-channelling nightsister, and put all three down without a scratch while Chatni battle-meditated from the Dara Dara — her last gift, she thought, to a dying Canderous. Struck down, Jera spent her own ghost to curse Tylo, and left Chatni a promise: "When they return, you will be the first to die." The crew took a copy of the Book of Shadows and Jera's saber. Canderous, kept alive by Chatni's art, did not die — and woke an addict, and a fanatically loyal friend.

Chapter XXII · c. 3940 BBY · Ilum & Tython

Bloodline and the Three Paths

Jera's logs · the destroyed artifacts · the light well

Jera's ship gave up its secrets: Chatni is of the main bloodline, and the missing candidates' power had been funnelled into the rival to make a weapon equal to her. The crew destroyed the worst of the dark artifacts on Ilum and memorised the Sith tablets. On Tython, Mikael counselled her toward a new medium of power beyond her own blood, which only deepens her tie to Dathomir. Two weeks in the light well laid three futures before her — Communion (the grazer), Self-empowerment (the predator), and Binding (a force of nature) — and a glimpse of an older light well on a world of red sand she has never seen.

Chapter XXIII · c. 3940 BBY · Bel Savas

Soah the Infernal One

the Gormak · the descent to the core · the galaxy-holocron

Beneath the ice, the Gormak — escaped slaves led by a chieftain and the timid techie Binion — laboured to free their people. The crew descended past force-crushed droids to the vault's heart and met Soah the Infernal One: last Rakatan emperor, jailed by his own kind for experimenting upon them, and content to stay jailed until his work is done. Fifty-six races and 26.4 million souls sleep there. Soah gave messages for Meetra, Kao Cen, and Satele, a comms device, and — through the chieftain's ancestral star-map — the revelation that the network of Rakatan temple-worlds is itself a galaxy-wide holocron, with the Foundry at its centre. Two hundred Gormak were carried out against a four-day life-support clock; the rest, and the question of Soah, wait.

Chapter XXIV · c. 3940 BBY · Tython

Xesh Returns

the woken ancient · the light well · lost lore

From the pods the crew brought Xesh — an ancient Force-being of the deepest Dathomiri past, who had never seen a bed nor eaten his fill. Brianna nearly killed him on sight; Meetra, trusting Chatni's intuition, granted him guarded sanctuary with restraints that can be triggered. He sleeps in meditation on the floor and searches, with desperate hope, for one named Shae. The light well drains him at every step, then claims him — Tython welcomes Tau back as its lost child. He carries lore long lost: the Singing Mountain Clan, the Spider sect who weave the dead into webs, the old cycle of becoming. Far below the temple sleeps Ood Bnar, a living-tree Jedi who may yet wake.

Chapter XXV · c. 3940 BBY · the dreaming

The Hunter, Unmasked

a clone collective · the Paragus fire · the move on GOTO

Dreamwalking with new mastery, Chatni rode the Hunter's mind and found not a man but a collective of clones — a twenty-seven-year Republic intelligence asset whose identity is so scrambled that each copy believes itself the original. They were behind the Paragus station explosion that gutted Telos' fuel supply, and the chaos on Csilla; they hate Jedi and Sith alike, yet are under orders to make sure the Jedi survive the Purge. Their next move is GOTO — to break his programming and turn him, and so resume their cloning. Chatni warned Meetra; Chancellor Cressa is said to hold a shutdown for GOTO. In the dead Gondian belt, a derelict still screams with a self-aware Hunter-voice that knows her name.

Chapter XXVI · c. 3940 BBY · Corellia

The Gas Cascades

a borrowed name · a borrowed dress · a rare and quiet night

Between shifts in the vault, Atton came to Bel Savas, offered Chatni his last name "as a friend," and carried her off to Corellia — to a fitted gown that cost more than a freighter (haggled down, because of course he knows the owner), to Selkath silk, and to a rare gas-cascade spectacle reached by a sideways glass elevator. Pazaak and easy banter; a deliberate turn away from passion toward something gentler. (The night is still unfolding.)

The Galactic Charter of Free Commerce the Free-Trade Writ

granted jointly by the Senate commerce committee & the seated Banking Guilds

The rare master instrument that renders all lesser licences unnecessary. The Free-Trade Writ grants its bearer the right to establish and conduct trade anywhere in signatory space — and, in exchange, vests them with authority to inspect cargoes and act against unlawful trade on behalf of the Trade Authority and the trade unions, in any sector. Its holder is at once the galaxy's freest merchant and a deputized guardian of its commerce: a private party clothed with public power. Beyond the Republic's recognition, however, it is only a fine piece of paper.
